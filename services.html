Business Registration
West Coast South Africa

Many small businesses on the West Coast operate without being formally registered — which means no business bank account, no ability to invoice as a company, and no legal protection for your personal assets. Getting registered is one of the most important things you can do for your business.

LaafWeb assists West Coast entrepreneurs and small business owners with CIPC company registration for a Private Company (Pty) Ltd — the most common business structure in South Africa. We handle the name search, reservation, and submission entirely via WhatsApp. You receive your CoR14.3 registration certificate digitally once CIPC has processed your application.

The R799 fee includes all CIPC government fees. Once registered, you can open a business bank account, issue professional invoices under your company name, and sign formal contracts. Note: we assist with the administrative process only — this is not legal advice.

POPIA Compliance
Privacy Policy South Africa

South Africa's Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) requires every website that collects personal information to have a Privacy Policy. This includes any site with a contact form, WhatsApp button, or email link — which is almost every business website. Non-compliance can result in fines of up to R10 million or imprisonment.

Most West Coast small businesses don't have a Privacy Policy on their website — not because they're dishonest, but because they don't know they need one. LaafWeb writes POPIA-compliant Privacy Policies specifically for your business — not generic copy-paste templates, but documents that reflect your actual data practices, services, and contact methods.

We also write Terms & Conditions that protect your business from payment disputes and set clear expectations with clients. Both documents are written based on information you provide about your business via WhatsApp, and are added directly to your website.

  • Terms & Conditions to protect you from disputes

Do I need a POPIA Privacy Policy on my South African business website?
Yes. Under POPIA (Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013), any South African website that collects personal information — including contact forms, WhatsApp buttons, or email links — is legally required to have a Privacy Policy. LaafWeb writes and adds POPIA-compliant Privacy Policies to your website for R350 once-off. A bundle of Privacy Policy + Terms & Conditions is R600.
