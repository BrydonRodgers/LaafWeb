Step 2 — Set Up Your Google Business Profile (Free)

A Google Business Profile is the listing that appears on Google Maps and in the "map pack" at the top of local search results. It's completely free and it's arguably more important than your website for local searches.

When someone in Saldanha searches "car wash near me" or "plumber Vredenburg", the first thing they see is a map with three business listings. Those listings are Google Business Profiles. If you're not there, you don't exist to that customer.

Setting one up takes about 20 minutes. Go to business.google.com, sign in with a Google account, and follow the steps. You'll need to verify your business — Google usually does this by sending a postcard to your address with a code, which takes about a week. Some businesses can verify instantly via phone or video.

  • Add your correct business name — exactly as it appears on your signage
  • Choose the right category — be specific (e.g. "Plumber" not just "Contractor")
  • Add your opening hours — including whether you're open on weekends
  • Upload at least 5 photos — inside your shop, your work, your team
  • Add your website URL — links your GBP to your website for stronger local SEO
  • Write a description — mention your town and what makes you different
💡 West Coast tip

Mention nearby towns in your description — e.g. "Serving Langebaan, Saldanha, and Vredenburg." Google uses this to show your business in searches from those areas even if you're based in just one of them.

Step 3 — Set Up WhatsApp Business

Most of your customers are already on WhatsApp — and it's how they prefer to communicate. WhatsApp Business is a free app that turns your regular WhatsApp into a proper business tool. It takes about 10 minutes to set up and makes a big difference to how professional you look.

Download WhatsApp Business from the App Store or Google Play. Sign in with your business number and set up your business profile — add your business name, category, opening hours, address, and a short description of what you do.

The most useful features to set up right away:

  • Away message — automatically replies when you're closed, e.g. "Thanks for your message! We're currently closed but will get back to you first thing tomorrow."
  • Greeting message — automatically sends when someone messages you for the first time
  • Quick replies — set up shortcuts for messages you send often (your price list, directions, booking link)
  • Catalogue — add your services or products so customers can browse before they message

Step 4 — Make Sure You're POPIA Compliant

This one catches a lot of small business owners off guard. South Africa's Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) requires any website that collects personal information — including contact forms, WhatsApp buttons, or email links — to have a Privacy Policy. Without one, you're technically non-compliant.

It's not something to stress about, but it is something to sort out before you launch your site. A proper Privacy Policy tells your customers what information you collect and how you use it. It builds trust and protects you legally.
